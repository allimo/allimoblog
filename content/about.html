Hi! I’m Allison. I do data stuff.

Career

Currently I’m a data analyst at CARFAX. My department handles inbound data that external sources provide to CARFAX; I build tools and automate tasks to help source analysts do their jobs better. I get to play with R all day and help other people use it more confidently.

Prior to CARFAX, I was a government contractor supporting the Federal Aviation Administration’s national airspace system. I started out doing administrative tasks, but as I automated a lot of them away with “fancy” spreadsheet formulas, I got to do more interesting stuff. Supporting the the Time-Based Flow Management system, I established a standardized process for sustainment analysis and reporting of reliability, maintainability, and availability metrics. My spreadsheets could ingest basic data output from a database and automatically handled formatting and generating the Excel charts required for monthly, quarterly, and annual reports. Later, I got to collaborate with air traffic control subject-matter experts to research, develop, and guide facility managers to create the next generation of air traffic contingency plans.

I’ve also worked in science education, technical writing, and a little bit of research astronomy. There’s a common thread there: I really love finding ways to make challenging information clear and manageable.

Education

I earned a Bachelor of Science from MIT, where I studied Earth, Atmospheric, and Planetary Sciences (focusing in planetary science & astronomy) with a minor in physics, and graduated in 2009.

I completed a masters degree in data analytics from George Mason University in 2017.

Community

Destination Imagination is a nonprofit that creates project-based team challenges and tournaments, with the goal to inspire the next generation of innovators, leaders, and creative problem solvers. I participated in the program as a student, and have seen firsthand the confidence that kids gain when they’re given the freedom to own their ideas and the power to make them a reality. I first became an adult volunteer with DI in 2010, and haven’t stopped since.

In 2014, I was a founding member of the Board of Directors for Northern Virginia DI, a region of the DI Virginia affiliate. I help organize and run the annual regional tournament; as a Regional Challenge Master, I train and lead a team of adult volunteer Appraisers who assess and score teams’ solutions to each year’s Technical Challenge, and as Head Appraiser, I help team members and team managers interpret the challenge rules, their team’s scores, and the Appraisers’ feedback. I also appraise at the Virginia statewide affiliate tournament, which hosts teams that advance from each region and selects which teams will represent the state at the Global Finals tournament.

Hobbies

Sometimes I paint portraits of pets.
